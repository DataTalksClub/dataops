# Playbook for promoting courses at DTC

## Summary

## Content

This is a playbook with templates and instructions to follow for promoting DTC free courses. You can learn more about them here: [A Guide to Free Online Courses at DataTalks.Club](https://datatalks.club/blog/guide-to-free-online-courses-at-datatalks-club.html)

### Activities to promote the course

### 1 Pre-course Q&A (2 weeks before the course start)

#### Ceth part

You need to create an event following the process for the webinar event as usual. Here is the information you’ll need

Date: Two weeks before the Course start date (Example: if the course starts on Sept 16, this event should be on Sep 2)

Title: \[COURSE NAME\] \[YEAR\] Pre-Course Live Q&A (Example: LLM Zoomcamp 2024 Pre-Course Live Q&A)

Guest: Usually with Alexey, but other instructors can be invited

Description of the event: this will be created by Valeriia

#### Valeriia

Templates of the description:

- [Q&A about LLMs Zoomcamp (event description)](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1n5VljKvqfGkC484RIUQbpmrioW9sOSZ4WB04VVxs5Kw/edit?usp=sharing)

### 2 Social media posts on Alexey’s accounts and DTC’s accounts (Start first posts one month before the course start)

Alexey and DTC have accounts on Twitter and Linkedin.

Posts:

- Announce the course start ±1 month before the start (Examples: [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/agrigorev_learn-machine-learning-engineering-in-4-months-activity-7225051800482050048-W9dh](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/agrigorev_learn-machine-learning-engineering-in-4-months-activity-7225051800482050048-W9dh) , [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/datatalks-club_a-new-cohort-of-the-ml-zoomcamp-starts-on-activity-7226852079267459072-nEne](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/datatalks-club_a-new-cohort-of-the-ml-zoomcamp-starts-on-activity-7226852079267459072-nEne) )

- Announce the Q&A webinar when the event is ready on lu.ma
- Announce the course start ±2 weeks before the start (You can be creative here and start with an educational content about the topic of the course or give some resources or make an interesting carousel about the topic)

- Feedback posts ±1 week before the start
  - Start a flashmob. Ask students to write about their experience in the comments

  - Find some feedback on social media from the students and also make posts about it

### 3 Other ideas to promote the course

- Reach out to top LinkedIn influencers in \[the topic of the course\] for course promotion

- Promote the course in relevant LinkedIn, Facebook, Discord, Slack groups and communities. Promote on HackerNews, Reddit, Quora
