Materials from the guest (for preparing questions):

A bit of background on me but the short version is that before Kestra, I used to build open source programs for students - basically a way for them to get experience contributing to large projects to help them start their careers. Because of this, I am a big fan of open source.

More detail:

Studied Computer Science at university and went to lots of hackathons building wacky projects (https://devpost.com/wrussell1999) in 24 hours.

When COVID-19 came around, was part of the driving force in turning hackathons into digital events.

Through that I got involved with building an open source development program for students as a lot of them lost internships and this was a good way to get experience building on big projects

Worked on that originally part time as a student but eventually transitioned full time and ran it for 4 years.

Worked with some interesting projects including Apache Airflow, Meta FAIR projects, Python pallets projects, Jupyter Lab projects and more.

After doing it and running various different programs in Web3, Production Engineering etc, I decided I wanted to move into a more technical role where I can use the knowledge and skills I gained working with so many open source projects.

Joined Kestra as a Developer Advocate - a product that has lots of flexibility and complexity and without some proper education will be impossible for people to learn.

Made lots of video testimonials for the MLH Fellowship as well as freelance videos for hackathons so naturally video production at Kestra made a lot of sense.
