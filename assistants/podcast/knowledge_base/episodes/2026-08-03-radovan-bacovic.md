Where does this story begin for you?

Radovan Bacovic:

Who or what first pulled you toward the topic?

Radovan Bacovic: It was a problem nobody wanted to take and own. Was attracted by a messy, sprawling, high-stakes pipeline with no clear owner. That first time I took one apart, understood it, and rebuilt it so it actually ran reliably, that was the pull. Happy to share my experience on the early days of Data Engineering and my project in a large Dutch telekom company

Which events, successes, or stumbles taught you the most?

Radovan Bacovic: The tool choices, without a doubt. I've watched teams, including my own, fall in love with a new framework before asking what problem they were actually solving. Shiny tools have a gravitational pull in this field, and I've got several stories of pipelines that became monuments to technical ambition rather than business value. Those failures taught me more than any success did. Let's not forget to mention I am failing daily.

Is there a theme that keeps recurring in your work or study?

Radovan Bacovic: Most data problems are people and process problems and not a technical issue. The broken pipeline usually has a broken handoff behind it. The missing data usually has a missing owner behind it. Happy to mention a boring solution example from GitLab

Who might recognize their own journey in yours?

Radovan Bacovic: Wide audience, as data is everywhere and there is something for everyone in it. Remember 2015. One expert said: data volume will grow 150x year on year, and the number of real data and AI experts will grow 5% per year. This is our chance, isn’t it?

Whose path resembles your early struggles?

Radovan Bacovic:

What 3-5 main stops, projects, or challenges mark the path—listed in order?

Radovan Bacovic:

AccountIQ - talk to your own data in AI fashion - put AI project at scale

An AI-based data product with processing Zendesk data - put AI in production on a smaller scale. Small effort with a huge impact

Streamlit Application framework - pioneering the Streamlit deployment

GitLab itself - 50m users, one of the biggest Postgres instances in the world, open source…

Was there a detour that almost changed your direction?

Radovan Bacovic: I was offered a move into management several times. What pulled me back was a pretty simple realisation: the problems I find genuinely interesting are technical, and the leverage I have comes from being deep in the work, not above it. And I see can contribute more as an IC - why lose a good engineer to get a mediocre manager?

What’s your current role, and what single experience ties you to this theme?

Radovan Bacovic: I'm a Staff/Principal Data Engineer. In brief, a high overview and strategic and architectural decisions one day and diving deeply into the code another day

What facts link present-you to this topic? Also, I am a regular international public speaker and find it super interesting to live several parallel lives

Radovan Bacovic: At the Staff level, my job is increasingly about helping other engineers avoid the mistakes I've already made, especially the tool-first, problem-second trap. The fact that I've lived through enough of these patterns across enough teams to recognise them early makes the role meaningful now.

If listeners remember one idea next week, what should it be?

Radovan Bacovic: Don't be scared to try and to fail
